    FOREX DICTIONARY

  • Ask Price: The lowest price a seller is willing to accept for a currency pair.

  • Bid Price: The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a currency pair.

  • Pip:"The smallest price movement in a currency pair, usually 0.0001 for most pairs."

  • Spread: The difference between the bid and ask price of a currency pair.

  • Leverage: Borrowed funds that allow traders to control larger positions with a smaller deposit.

  • Lot: "A standardized unit size for trading, usually 100,000 units for a standard lot."

  • Margin: The amount of money required to open or maintain a trading position.

  • Stop Loss: An order placed to automatically close a trade at a predetermined loss level.

  • Take Profit: An order placed to automatically close a trade at a predetermined profit level.

  • Bull Market: A market condition where prices are rising.

  • Bear Market: A market condition where prices are falling.

  • Currency Pair: "A pair of currencies traded in the forex market, e.g., EUR/USD."

  • Base Currency: The first currency in a currency pair.

  • Quote Currency: "The second currency in a currency pair, which is quoted against the base currency."

  • Long Position: A buy trade where the trader expects prices to rise.

  • Short Position: A sell trade where the trader expects prices to fall.

  • Stop Order: An order that activates only when the price reaches a specified level.

  • Slippage: The difference between the expected and actual trade execution price.

  • Liquidity: The ease with which a currency pair can be bought or sold without affecting its price.

  • Hedging: A strategy used to offset potential losses in one trade by opening another.

  • Scalping: A short-term trading strategy aiming for small profits from rapid trades.

  • Day Trading: A trading strategy that closes all trades within the same day.

  • Technical Analysis: Analyzing historical price charts and patterns to predict future movements.

  • Support Level: A price level where buying interest is strong enough to prevent further decline.

  • Trend: The general direction of price movement in the market.

  • Breakout: When price moves beyond a defined support or resistance level.

  • Candlestick: A type of price chart used in forex trading.

  • Fibonacci Retracement:: A tool used to identify potential support and resistance levels.

  • RSI: "Relative Strength Index, a momentum indicator measuring overbought or oversold conditions."

  • Swap: The interest paid or earned for holding a forex position overnight.

  • Carry Trade: A strategy of profiting from interest rate differentials between two currencies.

  • CFD: Contract for Difference, a derivative allowing traders to speculate on price movements."

  • Lot Size: "The size of a trading position, typically measured in standard, mini, or micro lots."

  • Risk Management: A strategy to minimize trading losses by controlling risk exposure.

  • Equity: "The total value of a trader’s account, including unrealized profits/losses."

  • Balance: The total amount of money in a trading account before open trades.

  • Drawdown: The reduction of account equity due to losing trades.

  • Margin Call: A broker notification that additional funds are required to maintain open positions.

  • Stop Out Level:

    The level at which a trader’s positions are automatically closed due to low margin.
